# AEGIS-R Laboratory

### **A**nalytics for **E**arthquake and **G**eohazard **I**nfrastructure **S**ilience & **R**esilience


Welcome to the **AEGIS-R Laboratory** at the **Department of Civil, Structural and Environmental Engineering**, **University at Buffalo, The State University of New York**.

The AEGIS-R Laboratory develops analytical, computational, and data-driven methods to better understand earthquakes and geohazards and improve the resilience of civil infrastructure. Our research combines earthquake engineering, geotechnical engineering, seismology, computational mechanics, statistics, machine learning, and remote sensing to develop the next generation of hazard assessment and risk mitigation tools.

Our mission is to advance the science of earthquake and geohazard engineering while educating the next generation of researchers and engineers through rigorous, impactful, and collaborative research.



## Research

Our work spans the complete hazard-to-risk framework, integrating field observations, physical models, and data-driven methods to better characterize natural hazards and their effects on infrastructure.

Current research areas include:

- **Seismic Hazard and Risk**
  - Non-ergodic ground-motion modeling
  - Bayesian inference and uncertainty quantification
  - Site characterization and site response

- **Surface Fault Rupture Hazard**
  - Fault displacement hazard analysis
  - Remote sensing and geospatial analytics

- **Computational Methods**
  - Computational mechanics
  - Scientific computing and machine learning

- **Experimental and Field Research**
  - Large-scale physical testing
  - Post-earthquake reconnaissance

- **Infrastructure Resilience**
  - Performance-based earthquake engineering
  - Resilient infrastructure systems

## Projects

Research within AEGIS-R is organized around interdisciplinary projects that combine theoretical developments, computational modeling, field observations, and engineering applications. Our projects range from fundamental methodological research to practical tools that support engineers, infrastructure owners, and public agencies in improving resilience against natural hazards.

## People

### Principal Investigator

#### Grigorios (Greg) Lavrentiadis
**Assistant Professor**  
Department of Civil, Structural and Environmental Engineering  
University at Buffalo, The State University of New York

Dr. Lavrentiadis leads the AEGIS-R Laboratory, where his research focuses on earthquake hazard and risk analysis through the integration of physics-based models, statistical learning, and computational methods. His interests include non-ergodic ground-motion modeling, probabilistic seismic hazard analysis, surface fault rupture, site response, remote sensing, and uncertainty quantification, with the goal of improving the resilience of civil infrastructure to natural hazards.

**Research Interests**
- Non-ergodic ground-motion modeling
- Seismic hazard and risk analysis
- Surface fault rupture hazard
- Site response and geotechnical earthquake engineering
- Bayesian statistics and machine learning
- Remote sensing and geospatial analytics

### Graduate Students

#### Saba Marmarchinia
**Ph.D. Student**  
Department of Civil, Structural and Environmental Engineering  
University at Buffalo

Saba's research focuses on probabilistic fault displacement hazard analysis and the statistical characterization of surface fault rupture. Her work aims to develop next-generation methodologies for estimating the hazard associated with principal and distributed fault ruptures and their implications for the seismic design of critical infrastructure.

- Probabilistic fault displacement hazard analysis
